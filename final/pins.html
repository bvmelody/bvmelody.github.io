Pins!

My personal OG, the first things I started collecting, enamel pins! These are in my opinion the best option to start collecting as they are incredibly easy to find in decent quality! There are thousands, perhaps hundreds of thousands of unique pin designs around the world, so it is incredibly easy to find pins you would like to display! But there are a few things you should keep in mind! Mainly regarding Specific categories of pins that might have some unique qualities that may make them more complex and/or difficult to collect.

An Inexhaustive List of Pin Categories:

Pin Categories Summary Difficulties of Collecting
Lapel Pins The most common form of pin - not often part of a larger collection Found in many stores, as freebees, or as singles
Disney Trading Pins Pins made for Disney properties and website, sold directly by disney rather than as Licensed product, thousands of different pins
  • Regular series pins are rather easy to collect as several collections will be availabel for several years
  • Limited Edition: More difficult to collect, often only available in the parks and in limited number
  • Limited Release: Much more difficult to collect, often only available in parks for a limited amount of time (usually ending within a year, though often shorter)
Official Space Pins Pins associated with various Space Programs around the world, with both limited and public sets, both commemorative and general releases
  • Limited Sets: Often only available for a short time or were only given out to individuals involved in the mission
  • Public Sets: Available for sale at official websites, mostly commemorative or public mission pins, or being a logo pin for the associated agency
  • Soviet Space Pins: Much harder to collect due to transport costs and sheer number available
Soviet-Era Pins Wide range of pins given out to Citizens and Soldiers of the USSR for commemorative events, awards, or anniversaries
  • Range in difficulty from easy to find to damn near impossible
  • The sheer number of pins produced makes it difficult to complete an entire Soviet Pin Collection
  • Pins for nearly everything, and not often well documented in English, most documentation is in Russian(and other eastern European languages) and hasn't been translated
  • Very few reliable resources for information regarding the thousands of unique pin designs, and no centralized documentation
Limited Release Pins Often made by independent creators - often requiring preordering before manufacture
  • Often available from fan-sites or from Etsy
  • Often produce the number pre-ordered
  • If you miss the preorder it's almost impossible to get the pin without either buying one off of someone else or trading
