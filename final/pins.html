Pins!

My personal OG, the first things I started collecting, enamel pins! These are in my opinion the best option to start collecting as they are incredibly easy to find in decent quality! There are thousands, perhaps hundreds of thousands of unique pin designs around the world, so it is incredibly easy to find pins you would like to display! But there are a few things you should keep in mind! Mainly regarding Specific categories of pins that might have some unique qualities that may make them more complex and/or difficult to collect.

Hard Enamel vs Soft Enamel

Likely the most common qualifier you will see when pin collecting is whether a pin is hard or soft enamel. Now, what does this mean? Well it is rather simple and easy to tell the differenc between the two!

Large Categories:

Lapel Pins

A very common form of pin that is often sold in singles in stores. Named for the fact they are designed to be worn on a jacket lapel. Though it doesn't have to be. For instance I often wear a pin on my shirt collar or pocket. This category actually encompasses most pins as even those that I would never consider wearing on a lapel, will be called "lapel pins" on the packaging. This isn't a knock on them or false advertising, it's just a convenient name that most people will understand.

It's also quite common the the United States to see politicians where an American Flag pin on their suit lapel, initially started by GOP candidates in the 1970 election, and would come to national attention thanks to Richard Nixon giving an order to all his staff to wear a flag pin in order to show their belief in their "silent majority". This trend would wane somewhat until the gulf war and 9/11 where the pins returned in force. Nowadays it's difficult to find politicians regularly going with the pin on the campaign trail.

Disney Trading Pins

One of the most well known pin collections is the various official Disney Trading Pin series! While pins had always existed in the Disney parks, the official pin trading program wasn't. Initially started in 1999 for the Millenium Celebration, the Disney Pin Trading program has been operating ever since! Often with incredibly dedicated fans! Including myself!

There are hundreds of collections available, from the huge variety of Disney Princess pins, to the sheer number of Mickey Mouse pins. From the hundreds of Star Wars pins, to the early Hannah Montana Pins.

Olympic Pins

For over 100 years the Olympic Games have been making pins for the games. While initially only for ease of identifying officials and organizers, has since become a phenomenon known as the "unofficial event" of the Olympics. The Olympic pin trading scene makes thousands of pins every year for the event, pins for specific olympians, nations, sports, etc. They are both a category that can be hard to collect when intially released, but can get progressively easier as te pins are slowly sold secondhand online. Or traded by those who went to the olympics.

Eastern-Bloc Pins

Oh where to begin? Since the fall of the Eastern-Bloc in the early 90s the Pin collecting for that era has exploded. The nations that fell under the Soviet's control are well known for the insane number of pins that they produced during the era. For everything from civil service, military accolades, to even general public works and groups, there were pins for nearly everything. While of course you have the "lenin face on a red flag" that is so common it's near impossible to not find them, you also have pins that were only produced in limited numbers in only one country that can be hellish to find.

There are two difficulties with collecting pins from this area of the world, and time period of their societies. The first one being that shipping said pins halfway across the world can be incredibly expensive depending on which seller you are buying from. And with the new tariffs in effect, who knows how much those pins will be going for in a few months? The second issue is less practical and more personal. How many people will be put off by the fact that you collect these pins? Especially people who suffered under those regimes can be quite sensitive about the topic and just flat out stating "I collect these" can draw out responses that can be accurate in opinion, if misunderstanding the purpose. Needless to say, just remember not to be careful about making sure people understand you are collecting for the love of the game, rather than out of love for the regime.

Government Pins

This is a category that is relatively easy to start collecting, but damn near impossible to get a full collection. There are thousands of pins issued to government employees as commemoration of their work or of their agency's history. Whether it be civil or military, these pins are widely known and relatively easy to collect. Though some are harder than others, especially pins that are no longer produced or are only given to members of the group they were made to commemorate, most famously smaller military units. I've heard of some enamel pins created to commemorate members of the seal teams, but I have yet to see an image of one. Needless to say I'd like to find one, and if you do find one, send us a pic on our about us page! We'd love to see it!

Company Pins

Similar to government pins, these pins are given about by companies to their employees, typically commemorating a time period or department within said company. The most well known of these are the McDonald's employee pins which are given out to the staff of their restaurants for each new toy lineup. Everything from Toy Story to Pokemon, every franchise has a pin that has been at McDonald's.

There's also many companies that give their higher ups pins of the company logo, such as CVS, where my coworker got one from a local area manager. Needless to say, I am extremely jealous. But who knows? Maybe I'll find one sometimes soon!

What Should You Collect?

Honestly? Whatever you would like to! I started by purchasing two pins at a regional tournament! You don't need to collect the rarest, or the coolest pins! Just collect the pins that make you happy! But also, if you're looking for a particlar collection to start with, start trying to collect all of the pins in a random pin box that you can find in pop culture stores like Hot-Topic or BoxLunch! These are often affordable and gives you something to get a "full collection" of without spending as much up front!
