Pins!

My personal OG, the first things I started collecting, enamel pins! These are in my opinion the best option to start collecting as they are incredibly easy to find in decent quality! There are thousands, perhaps hundreds of thousands of unique pin designs around the world, so it is incredibly easy to find pins you would like to display! But there are a few things you should keep in mind! Mainly regarding Specific categories of pins that might have some unique qualities that may make them more complex and/or difficult to collect.

Hard Enamel vs Soft Enamel

Likely the most common qualifier you will see when pin collecting is whether a pin is hard or soft enamel. Now, what does this mean? Well it is rather simple and easy to tell the differenc between the two!

Soft Enamel Hard Enamel
Raised metal Edges Flat surface
Wide variety of finishes Limited variety of finishes
Less durable and more prone to scratches More durable and less prone to scratches
Inexpensive and easier to produce More expensive and more involved production process
Able to accomodate more detail Unable to support finer details

A very common form of pin that is often sold in singles in stores. Named for the fact they are designed to be worn on a jacket lapel. Though it doesn't have to be. For instance I often wear a pin on my shirt collar or pocket. This category actually encompasses most pins as even those that I would never consider wearing on a lapel, will be called "lapel pins" on the packaging. This isn't a knock on them or false advertising, it's just a convenient name that most people will understand.

It's also quite common the the United States to see politicians where an American Flag pin on their suit lapel, initially started by GOP candidates in the 1970 election, and would come to national attention thanks to Richard Nixon giving an order to all his staff to wear a flag pin in order to show their belief in their "silent majority". This trend would wane somewhat until the gulf war and 9/11 where the pins returned in force. Nowadays it's difficult to find politicians regularly going with the pin on the campaign trail.

One of the most well known pin collections is the various official Disney Trading Pin series! While pins had always existed in the Disney parks, the official pin trading program wasn't. Initially started in 1999 for the Millenium Celebration, the Disney Pin Trading program has been operating ever since! Often with incredibly dedicated fans! Including myself!

There are hundreds of collections available, from the huge variety of Disney Princess pins, to the sheer number of Mickey Mouse pins. From the hundreds of Star Wars pins, to the early Hannah Montana Pins.
